I used to despise the saying, “Everything happens for a reason.” If everything does happen for a reason, then what is it? Why does it have to be kept in secrecy and leave me in timeless pondering? Why does it have to make me stay up all night overlooking everything I did?

Then I remember the quote, “The more you know, the more you suffer.” In the act of knowing more do we kill ourselves. We get stuck in a loop of doubt and regret. Everything does happen for a reason, it's just not meant for us to know, and that's a good thing. Only by letting the things beyond our control slip away from our very hands do we free ourselves from the torment worrying, for ignorance is a bliss—ignorance is peace.

Even greatest questions in science remain unanswered: “What lies at the bottom of the ocean?” “What lies beyond the great darkness of the universe?” “Why do we exist?” Had we known the answers to these questions, what would we do with them?

It is human to be curious and wonder, to question the inner workings of existence and purpose, but why do we spend so much unhealthy time worrying about these things that [contribute nothing for our growth?]
