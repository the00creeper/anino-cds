The European Union provides humanitarian aid to countries and people in need, both within Europe and overseas. One significant instance is the Ukraine-Russia conflict which still persists to this day. Since the beginning of the conflict in February 2022, the EU and its member states has provided around €134 billion (₱8.1 trillion) worth of military and humanitarian aid in support for Ukraine and its people [^1]. The EU has even handed out support to the victims of the Mindanao armed conflict here in the Philippines that has been happening since 2012. In 2023, the EU provided €4.4 million (₱265.9 million) to the Philippines, including €1.5 million (₱90.6 million) in support of people affected by the protracted conflict, and is one of the largest international donors to the Bangsamoro peace process [^2]. 

Prominent examples of transnational activism in action include:
1. The Anti-Globalization Movement: a movement against governments, international institutions, and private firms [^3].
2. Movement against genetically modified organisms (GMOs): a movement that fights for the health risk awareness of human GMO consumption [^3].
3. Migration as a religious obligation: moving to a different country for religious reasons [^4].
