Lithosphere
It provides a solid surface for terrestrial organisms. Moreover, it also contains the minerals and natural resources that are necessary for life, such as the nutrients that plants need. Fuels such as coal, petroleum, and natural gas are also found in the litosphere.

Hydrosphere
It constitutes to the bodies of water on Earth, in which water sustains all life in it. The very first organisms formed on the hydrosphere, and from there, life grew and flourished to what we know today. It serves as the habitat to marine animals in freshwater, saltwater and other aquatic ecosystems.

Atmosphere
It provides the oxygen most living organisms need to metabolize. It also protects the Earth from the harmful UV rays of the Sun. Moreover, it regulates the temperature of the Earth to be habitable.
