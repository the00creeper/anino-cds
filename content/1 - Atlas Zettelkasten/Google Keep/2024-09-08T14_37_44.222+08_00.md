
I am a hard worker and academic achiever in school.
I am a person who fears God and respects others.
I am a responsible and law-abiding citizen.

I am an ambivert.
My social battery runs out fast.
Oftentimes, I prefer to be alone.
I am a concerned perfectionist in certain fields.
I speak the truth about society.

People think I'm an extrovert.
People think I could socially engage all day.
People think I'd like to hang out.
People think I'm a perfectionist at everything.
People think I'm silent about the truth.

My parents think I'm an extrovert.
My friends also know I could socially engage all day.
My true friends know I sometimes reject hangouts.
My classmates/groupmates also think I'm a perfectionist at everything.
My internet friends know I'm straightforward with my opinions.

I believe the private self is more persistent. While it is true that others may know you better in some aspects, it is only you who can understand who you are at all times. People will come and go in your life—they may no longer know who you are once they're gone—but you will stay by yourself for as long as you can hold your stand, gaining a deeper understanding of yourself through thick and thin.

I agree with Geertz. He described ourselves as "animals" to signify that we would simply lose our minds had we confined ourselves as one singular being, rejecting a world that inevitably connects us together. We are born "animals" and "humanize" ourselves through time by immersing ourselves in culture.
