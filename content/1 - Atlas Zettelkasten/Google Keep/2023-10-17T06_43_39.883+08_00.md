
The story of Orpheus and Eurydice revolved around the concept of trust, in which Orpheus was unsuccessful in doing so. Orpheus was tested mentally and emotionally, and his concern and paranoia led to the demise of his loving wife—Eurydice. It's as if he was met with a dilemma, “Will I continue moving forward whilst uncertain that she's with me? Or will I catch a glimpse of her to assure that she's there? Will I obey my obligation or myself?” For this story, trust was central to the resolution of the conflict, and the consequences of his actions scarred Orpheus with pain and regret for the rest of his days. 

The story can be applied to the ways of love in real life. It has evidently proven that physical presence is not the sole assurance of love; but rather, trust and patience are more than enough to manifest the affection and mutualism required to express love and importance to someone.
