Aldrei Mercado here for a brief introduction of a position paper.

A position paper is an academic work that seeks to convince readers of the paper about a certain idea or a specific solution to a particular problem based on objective evidence. The purpose of a position paper is to persuade the readers into siding with the author's position about an issue in an attempt to completely disregard the opposing position.

Rosswel Mangunay here to discuss the different parts of a position paper.

Introduction
Introduce the topic, and then provide history or background of the issue. You may also describe the issue on-hand so do your research.

Body
State the opposing argument by briefly describing them. You may also include details that support the opposing claim. Then, after writing the counter argument, now write your supporting argument and give your point of view. Provide evidence that support your claim. Now you state your second argument followed by your third argument. Make sure to provide details that support the argument given.

Conclusion
Restate both sides of the argument, summarize the three arguments made in your essay. Lastly, provide a call for action.

List of References 
This section typically lists all the sources that have been cited within the document. It also helps readers locate and verify the information presented in the paper.

Aldrei Mercado here to discuss the guidelines and a share few tips in writing a position paper.

The title of your paper should clearly state the issue, where it occurs, who are involved, and your position on the matter.
“The Importance of Maintaining Traditional over Modern Methods of Learning in Facilitating Academic Activities in the Classroom”

On the introduction, immediately stating your position at the first sentences of the paragraph is not recommended.
“Through time, technology has changed so much, so much so that it has influenced our style of learning. Therefore using traditional learning methods, such as reading a book to gather information, is more effective and reliable than its modern screen-device counterpart.”

Instead, your introduction should first contain a “hooking” statement to draw your readers to pursue reading your work. This will serve as your topic sentence. Your position, or thesis statement, should be at the end of the introduction and is recommended to only be in one sentence.
“𝘛𝘩𝘦 𝘱𝘢𝘴𝘴𝘢𝘨𝘦 𝘰𝘧 𝘵𝘪𝘮𝘦 𝘤𝘰𝘪𝘯𝘤𝘪𝘥𝘦𝘥 𝘸𝘪𝘵𝘩 𝘵𝘩𝘦 𝘥𝘦𝘷𝘦𝘭𝘰𝘱𝘮𝘦𝘯𝘵 𝘰𝘧 𝘵𝘦𝘤𝘩𝘯𝘰𝘭𝘰𝘨𝘺 𝘪𝘯 𝘵𝘩𝘦 𝘸𝘰𝘳𝘭𝘥, 𝘸𝘩𝘪𝘤𝘩 𝘶𝘯𝘥𝘦𝘳𝘭𝘪𝘯𝘦𝘥 𝘪𝘵𝘴 𝘨𝘳𝘢𝘥𝘶𝘢𝘭 𝘤𝘩𝘢𝘯𝘨𝘦 (topic sentence). But not all the changes taking place today are for the good of everyone. Although many favor modern learning methods that use modern technology, there are still many obstacles and problems, especially if users do not know how to use or abuse it. 𝘜𝘴𝘪𝘯𝘨 𝘵𝘳𝘢𝘥𝘪𝘵𝘪𝘰𝘯𝘢𝘭 𝘭𝘦𝘢𝘳𝘯𝘪𝘯𝘨 𝘮𝘦𝘵𝘩𝘰𝘥𝘴, 𝘴𝘶𝘤𝘩 𝘢𝘴 𝘳𝘦𝘢𝘥𝘪𝘯𝘨 𝘢 𝘣𝘰𝘰𝘬 𝘵𝘰 𝘨𝘢𝘵𝘩𝘦𝘳 𝘪𝘯𝘧𝘰𝘳𝘮𝘢𝘵𝘪𝘰𝘯, 𝘪𝘴 𝘮𝘰𝘳𝘦 𝘦𝘧𝘧𝘦𝘤𝘵𝘪𝘷𝘦 𝘢𝘯𝘥 𝘳𝘦𝘭𝘪𝘢𝘣𝘭𝘦 𝘵𝘩𝘢𝘯 𝘪𝘵𝘴 𝘮𝘰𝘥𝘦𝘳𝘯 𝘴𝘤𝘳𝘦𝘦𝘯-𝘥𝘦𝘷𝘪𝘤𝘦 𝘤𝘰𝘶𝘯𝘵𝘦𝘳𝘱𝘢𝘳𝘵 (thesis statement).”

The body should contain the details that support your position. Present an argument from the opposing position.
“Tablets nowadays only need to be charged for a short period of time in order to operate. They can also utilize the full potential of the internet to browse for inquiries or use internet tools to aid in their comprehension of the lesson for the day…”

Then make a counterargument that completely contradicts the opposing position. Make sure to back up your counterarguments with sufficient evidence (e.g., statistics, examples, etc.) to further strengthen your claim.
“𝘐𝘯 𝘤𝘰𝘯𝘵𝘳𝘢𝘴𝘵 𝘵𝘰 𝘵𝘩𝘪𝘴, the average battery life of tablets is only around seven hours, depending on the unit and its model (Blackview, 2023). It is evident that this given time is still slightly less than the amount of school hours which is eight hours per day. 𝘐𝘯 𝘢𝘥𝘥𝘪𝘵𝘪𝘰𝘯 𝘵𝘰 𝘵𝘩𝘪𝘴, unlike tablets, books are uncompromisable, unhackable, and don't lag or slow down processing. Since it does not need to use the internet, books are not at risk of being hacked by opportunistic people, which prevents the students’ information from being stolen or leaked…”

The conclusion should not only summarize the entire text, but also restate your claim and offer a call-to-action on how the theory of your position can become practice.
“The government does not necessarily have to restrict the use of modern technology in learning. They only have to implement programs that prevent traditional methods from being obsolete.”

Lastly, end your paper by presenting a lasting statement that will leave your readers thinking.
“Books are the future, because the future does not necessarily mean advanced or modern; it could simply be something sustainable and reliable.”
