
We are now at the stage of our lives when we finally start to realize that we really are getting old. You see new pictures of your elementary school classmates and react to how different they look now. You see pictures of your past self and question, “Have I really grown that much?” Your family is slowly growing by the numbers. Your loved ones are slowly passing away. You'll soon graduate from college and take responsibility of joining the work force. You'll have a family of your own to accompany you for life. 

Despite the weight that the thought of the future holds, try taking a stop from time to time, recall the past—recall how it taught you to live. Cherish the moments that shaped you to become the great person you are now; moments of sorrow gave lessons, moments of joy gave memories. Look back at the old photos that captured your innocence as a child, the smile you wore with genuine happiness—the very smile that was yet to understand reality and the pain it brings. Learn to conquer life step by step, slow down. Life is not a race to be won. Time is short, but spend it right and it will last an eternity.

In a way, the more you know, the sadder you become; the more you understand, the more you doubt: “Will I ever achieve, once more, the genuine happiness I felt before I understood life?”
