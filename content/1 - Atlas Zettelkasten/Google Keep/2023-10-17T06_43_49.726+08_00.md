
The COVID-19 pandemic has truly been an oblivion to everyone. We were all just having a good time prior to its heavy impact. It has significantly proven the uncertainty of events and how unaware we are to what tomorrow may be. Two years have passed in a blink of an eye.

The pandemic shook the country's economy down to its very core; it was in shambles. As the nation underwent rigorous lockdown measures, unemployment rates skyrocketed and the masses were left jobless. My father was one of the many that lost their job. He used to work as the Supervisor at Waltermart Gapan's Gapan Bingo and his salary was sufficient enough to sustain our daily needs and more. Fortunately for us, despite losing his job, my father got employed as an agent in an internet provider company, since internet usage got noticeably more relevant during the times of quarantine; his new salary is, however, not always a good source of income to rely on as it is almost always barely enough.

As a student, the new normal mode of learning has not been a piece of cake. We are often met with multiple deadlines that were inconveniently put to be passed on the same day. Despite having sufficient time to do so, I often lack motivation to finish anything as knowing the fact that finishing the current batch of activities alone will almost instantaneously create more—since the day of passing old modules and receiving the new ones is the same day.
