
Gear Up

Wear the proper equipment needed for the operation. Safety goggles, hand gloves, face masks, and lab coats are a necessity in performing electronic activities. Avoid wearing loose clothing.

Listen

If you are not trained or asked to use a specific electronic component in class, do not touch it. Follow all the procedures properly and pay attention to your teacher or instructor.

Assess

Check your extension cords and plugs — a torn jacket, exposed wire at the plug or missing a "ground" pin are quick checks to ensure that the cord is in good working condition.

Quick checks to ensure that everything is in good working condition. Check your tools for damaged cords, missing ground pins, or exposed wires. Test all circuits to ensure the absence of voltage with the proper meter. 

Understand Hazards

Electrical currents can cause shocks, fires, explosions, and burns which can be fatal or cause serious injuries. Follow all cautions and warnings marked on the equipment. Do not overload circuits.

Tidying Up

Clean up spills immediately. Always keep your work area clean. Organize your tools and equipment accordingly. Report all unsafe conditions, accidents, and injuries to your instructor.
