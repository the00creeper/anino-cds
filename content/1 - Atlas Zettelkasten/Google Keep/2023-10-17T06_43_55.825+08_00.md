
The text demonstrated a great analogy to the saying, "Doing philosophy involves action."

I believe that philosophy is a process that involves two steps: Evaluation and Application. Evaluation is where we stop for a moment, drop everything down, and think. It is where we reflect upon our actions: our past mistakes, how we treated people, how we ignored our problems, and so on. We then try to find a lesson to such actions: what we could've done in the past, and what we should do in the future to avoid the same mistakes from happening again.

The absence of Application in philosophy voids the reflections concluded during Evaluation. It hinders change and our willingness to become better people. Information is useless if we do not use it; so is philosophy if we do not apply it.
