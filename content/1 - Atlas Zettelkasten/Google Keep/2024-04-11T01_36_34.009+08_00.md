
• Teenage pregnancies among Filipino women—15 to 19 years old—fell from 8.6 percent in 2017 to 5.4 percent in 2022, as stated by the Philippine Statistics Authority in its 2022 National Demographic and Health Survey.
• Childbearing in adolescence often causes a ripple effect, including increased risks for poor health outcomes for both mother and child, and often lower educational attainment and employability.
• The legal age of consent in the Philippines was raised from 12 to 16 in 2022 but written parental consent is still needed for children aged 18 and below to access contraceptives.
• Mothers under 15 are also twice as likely to die from complications in pregnancy or childbirth as women between the ages of 20 and 30.
• Pregnancy during teenage years is associated with a higher risk of health problems such as preeclampsia, anemia, contracting STDs (sexually transmitted diseases), premature delivery, postpartum hemorrhage, and poor mental health outcomes.
