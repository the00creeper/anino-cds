
One peaceful afternoon in Barangay Marites, a young lady of demure status named Navia arrived home with a boy for the first time. Luzviminda, a bystander, witnessed the boy's entry into Navia's home. It is believed that the two are the only people in the house since Navia's parents have gone to run some errands. Strange noises can also be heard from their voices in the house. Luzviminda immediately called out for an emergency meeting involving the entire neighborhood, only to discuss that Navia must've been pregnant based on what she had heard. The news spread like wildfire throughout the entire barangay. The following day, Navia heard about the rumors about her and the boy. She exclaimed to Luzviminda that the noises she heard was from them practicing for their upcoming singing activity in school and that they were chosen to be partners despite not being talented at all in singing.

The elements of human communication were presented in the context of the passage as:
1. Sender: Luzviminda was the first sender of the message. Whoever receives the message also became the sender to other receivers.
2. Receiver: The people Luzviminda talked to were the receivers of the message.
3. Message: The message was that Navia was pregnant, which turned out to be false.
4. Channel: The message was transmitted to other people through vocal communication.
5. Feedback: Navia exposing the rumor to be false was the feedback.
