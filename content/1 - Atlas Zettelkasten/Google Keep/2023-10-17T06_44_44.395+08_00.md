
A prose is a literary piece which is written in the pattern of ordinary spoken language and within the common flow of conversation. It is derived from the Latin word prosa which means straightforward. A prose can be fiction or nonfiction. Fiction meaning imaginary and not based on history and fact, while nonfiction meaning based mainly on fact and relates information. Examples of fictional works include short stories, novels, novellas, fables, while examples of nonfictional works are biographies, journals, diaries, history, and any other factual medium.

A prose is made up of elements. The theme is the lesson or message of the story, characters that play and represent the work, the setting which is when or where the story takes place, and the plot which tells what happens in the story. The plot can be further broken down into sub-elements. The exposition is where the elements of the story are typically introduced, the rising action where the main character is in crisis and the conflict begins to unfold, the climax in which a major event occurs and the whole turning point of the story is unveiled, the falling action where the story slows down and works toward its end, and finally the resolution where any remaining issues resolve and the story ends.
