
I remember the last four days of sheer pressure and discouragement that our 10th-grade research defense brought like it was yesterday. Our study was about Stevia sugar and emphasized that it is a zero-calorie, all-natural sweetener—the perfect substitute for ordinary table sugar. 
It was Saturday, the 21st of May, 2022. We needed the weighing scale that our research adviser owns but forgot to bring it home after classes have been dismissed. It was later discovered that another group had already taken the weighing scale home to Brgy. San Carlos. We really needed that scale ASAP. Out of the pressure of not being able to finish the study in time, Pierre—my research co-member—decided to rush the weighing scale to San Carlos, in the middle of the night, with only him, his bike, and the darkness within. After retrieving the weighing scale, he brought it to my house, where I did what I had to do.
It was Tuesday, the 24th of May, a day before our research. To utilize efficiency, my three other groupmates went to Savemore to buy the Lipton box packaging upon which our product will be stored, while Dhenszy and I went to their house to finish the PowerPoint presentation. I then realized that I forgot to take pictures of the tools and methods that we performed for the research, which were laying at my house. I did what I had to do; I went back tome, took some pictures, and went back to Dhenszy's house.
