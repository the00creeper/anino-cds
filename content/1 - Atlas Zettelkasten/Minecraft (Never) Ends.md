In Minecraft, there are infinitely many ways to place a block. There are also infinitely many ways to place another block—be it adjacent, on top, or completely irrelevant to the first. This concept of having a **collective infinity** of ways to create something is the foundation of **Minecraft**.

Despite having a general progression and goal, I believed as a kid that there really is no end to Minecraft. There are infinitely many new ways to explore, build, and create anything in the game. But what does end Minecraft externally is when you **no longer** play the game. Be it finding a new game to play or just growing up and having no time to log in like I have **defeats** Minecraft.

I makes me sad since I used to think that Minecraft was endless; but it was me—the **very player** that gave life to the game, and my naturally growing human self, inevitable to face life’s responsibilities—that will put the game to its **demise**.
