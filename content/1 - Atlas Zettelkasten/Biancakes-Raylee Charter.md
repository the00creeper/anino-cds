SUMACAB, Philippines — The long-awaited [[Bianca Alexis Capagcuan|Bianca]] and [[Raylee de Guzman|Raylee]] reunion finally commenced this Monday on the first day of Charter Week in [[Nueva Ecija University of Science and Technology|NEUST Sumacab Campus]].

I met them at the Heroes’ Park and Raylee was a facilitator for the Street Painting competition.

Me and Bianca were separated and had deeptalks about our career paths, standing beside a tree shade, where I revealed to her that I chose Electrical Engineering because I was moved by other people’s opinions on what I should be. ^c78bd3

She, like everyone else, never expected me to be passionate about teaching and becoming a teacher and instead saw me in the engineering or medical field. ^d80a11

She considered stuying at Mapúa, but only as an architect under a condition made by her father, to which she denied.

Her first art competition was in 2nd Grade—at the time when she was in public school at San Fernando Sur Elementary School—which introduced her to the world of art.

She put her current program, BSIE (Bachelor of Science in Industrial Education), as her first choice in the NEUST application form over BS Architecture—while art-related, she had no interest in designing buildings. 

She made up her mind when she asked the Lord for a sign on which program to take at the Saint John Church in Cabiao on the day of filing the application form; the Lord responded when her favourite teacher from SJNPS (Saint John Nepomucene Parochial School) introduced her to his former students now studying BSIE, to which they showed her their art projects.

She really loves SJNPS; she had studied there for nine years since she was in 3rd Grade.

She claimed that SJNPS really prepares its students for the outside world, and that her teachers drastically improved her communication and socialization skills; she now considers herself an extrovert.

She even exclaimed that if SJNPS had college programs, she wouldn’t be here talking to me.

I quickly disagreed and exclaimed that clinging on so badly to things that comfort us is a hindrance to discovering what other beautiful things the world had prepared for us to see—something I had to go through a lot of pain to learn.

When she had a “failing” grade of 89 in senior high school Chemistry, her parents exploded; kinda ironic since her elder brother is a Chemistry teacher in a private school.

We finally decided to sit on the nearby shaded benches when it was no longer occupied.

She mentioned that she was amazed by me and [[Mhartyn Pierre Heactor Santiano|Pierre]]’s photography skills when we posted our “Kapayaan Bago Ang Sakuna” picture of the [[San Isidro]] Plaza before a big storm.

Her female senpai from LSG (Local Student Government), who to me looked very approachable and capable, sat down with us to take a break from facilitating; she shortly left after.

We argued between the acts reading manga, which she prefers, and watching anime, which I prefer.

I defended my anime side—in a Duterte voice, as per her request—stating that we consume media and critique them with respect to our field of interests: an artist would be drawn to the visuals of the scene—from its lighting to its color theory—such that an animator would be drawn to the fluidity and humanness of the characters in motion. As a musician, I am drawn to the music; the original soundtracks are one of the most important elements for a successful emotion-evoking show. It is one of the first things I notice and I would later research and analyze its composition had it caught my creative preferences.

She defended her side stating that the imagination becomes alive when reading, and that you actively recreate the scenes originating from the author's head into your own, from the sound effects to the emotion in the voices of the characters. To enjoy reading, all you need is a vivid imagination.

I went on and recommend to her an anime with one of the best original soundtracks, ‘Sound! Euphonium,’ to which I paid her 100 pesos (serial code: G907444) just to show how much I recommend it to anyone (she had read its manga before, what a lunatic).

[[Airish Jade Santos|Airish]] arrived out of nowhere, we took a picture, and she left.

Then sat down with us her two “friends,” Nichole and the other dude, best friends since childhood, whom both Raylee and Bianca hate (though Bianca pretends to be cool with Nichole).

Raylee took a break from from facilitating and the conversation suddenly became vulgar—typical male-to-male interactions.

The two friends seem to be cool with Raylee and Bianca casually cussing the n-word.

Yohann came and went shortly.

Raylee left to facilitate once more; Bianca revealed the story between the two.

Before that, I first went to time-out my index card at the open gym with her and we grabbed lunch at the Chowking stall at the central court. We went back to the Heroes’ Park but our seat was already occupied. Soon, the occupiers left and we took back what’s ours.
