# Rationale
- Started out as an idea while eating pizza.
- Datu Puti (cat) looks up on father, hoping he’d give him food. 
- As creatures with free will, my father, a human, could kick the cat if he wanted to, he could even do more violent things with the cat if he really wanted to.
- The cat, being inferior in size and physical strength to the human, is subjected the human’s mercy, oblivious to the possible heinous thoughts he might have.
- At the same time, look at beings larger than us—elephants, bears, giraffes, whales—they might also be having the same dark thoughts on us, especially those that are trained.
- Most of the time, they show no aggression, suggesting that they develop moral values that suppress instinct.
- Human aggression against smaller creatures have always existed. How they trample over ant nests, swat wandering spiders, crush cruising snails, etc.
- These smaller creatures are totally unaware of the existence of these humans ending their existence. How unaware they are of their impending doom.
- How oblivious these small creatures are that being seen would cost them their lives.

# Goal
- Create a children’s story that simulates this concept of morality.
- The story could be a fable.
- Our power as giants is responsibility.
