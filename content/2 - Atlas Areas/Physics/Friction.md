# Object at Rest
$F$ cancels $F_{S}$ such that $F-F_{S}=0$.
$F_{N}$ cancels $W$ such that $F_{N}-W=0$.
This is because at rest, the summation of all forces in an axis is equal to zero.
$$\sum{F}=0$$
On the $x$-axis, $F$ points to the positive $x$ direction, and $F_S$ points to the negative $x$ direction ($-F_S$). The sum of those two forces is zero if the object were to be at rest.
$$F+(-F_S)=0$$
On the $y$-axis, $F_N$ points to the positive $y$ direction, and $W$ points to the negative $y$ direction ($-W$). The sum of those two forces is zero if the object were to be at rest. $$
F_N+(-W)=0
$$
$$
F=F_S
$$ The maximum force before an object starts moving. If $F>F_S$, then the object will move.

Why is $W_{y}=\cos{\theta}$? Isn’t $y$ a component of $\sin$?
	This is because $W_{y}$ is adjacent to $W$. Think of rotating the sideways right triangle upright, you’ll find $W_{y}$ to be along the $x$-axis, making it $\cos{\theta}$.
